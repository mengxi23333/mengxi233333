What is Cyberpunk?

Cyberpunk is a term used to describe a genre of science fiction writing that has developed since the 1980s. To be sure, the term "CyberPunk" was basically first coined by Bruce Bethke in his 1983 science fiction novel Cyberpunk.

  1. Cyber:

    2. "Cyber" mainly refers to advanced technologies, especially information technology, cyberspace, artificial intelligence, and other concepts related to electronic devices and virtual reality. This section highlights the profound impact of technology on society and individuals, especially at the level of control, surveillance, and the integration of technology into human life." The "Cyber" element often presents a worldview that is highly technologically advanced but potentially morally and socially structurally flawed.

  2. Punk:

    3. The "Punk" component in "Cyberpunk" represents a spirit of resistance against the system and rebellion. It is not only a gesture against the dominant culture, but also a critique of power structures, social inequality, and the exploitation and marginalization caused by capitalist overdevelopment. The "Punk" element is often represented in stories by portraying marginal characters, anti-heroes, and people who live at the bottom of society and fight against the system.

  3. Cyberpunk:

    4. "Cyberpunk" depicts a future world with highly advanced technology but distorted social ethics and moral concepts, in which individuals and small groups seek survival and meaning in a dystopian society filled with advanced technology, showing a unique futuristic perspective and critical thinking. In this setting, the genre explores the complex relationships and possible futures of humans, technology, and society.
